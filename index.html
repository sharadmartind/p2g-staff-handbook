Pathways to Graduation (P2G)

Preparing for high school equivalency and beyond

This handbook provides clear expectations to ensure a productive and professional working environment for the school community. All staff are responsible for the content in its entirety. Use the navigation bar to explore the different chapters.

Chapter 1: Chancellor's Regulations

This section details the New York City Department of Education's Chancellor's Regulations, which are divided into four volumes covering various aspects of school operations and employee conduct.

  • Volume A Regulations: Address student-related issues, from admissions to promotion.
  • Volume B Regulations: Address school-based budgeting.
  • Volume C Regulations: Address employee issues, from hiring to termination.
  • Volume D Regulations: Address parent and community involvement.

Chapter 2: Staff Payroll and Attendance

Personnel Attendance - Policies and Procedures

  • Reporting Absences: Staff must report absences directly to their principal, AP, and school-based payroll secretary via email *prior to 7:00 AM* on the day of each absence. The type of absence must be indicated for proper coding.
  • Timekeeping: All payroll banks timekeeping, including Cybershift employees, will be managed and entered at the borough school levels. School Aides must use a timecard to punch in and out daily.
  • Substitute Teachers (SubCentral): Classroom teachers who need a substitute must contact the SubCentral System (718-935-6740 or online).
  • Weekly Attendance Forms: All P2G pedagogical staff and Paraprofessionals must submit an online weekly attendance form by 4:00 PM on Monday of the following week, listing all absences, late arrivals, and early departures.

Types of Leave and Documentation

  • Personal Days: Must be requested in advance and in writing to the Borough Principal or respective manager.
    • UFT members: Maximum of three per year.
    • UFT Paras: Maximum of three per school year.
    • School Aides: Maximum of one per school year.
  • Bereavement: An absence due to a death in your immediate family is a "non-attendance day" with full pay and is *not* deducted from your Cumulative Absence Record (CAR). Granted the day of death plus the next three calendar days (excluding weekends/holidays).
  • Lateness/Early Departure: Must be immediately notified to the Borough Principal or designee and can be deducted from CAR balances.
  • Absence Documentation:
    • OP 198 form: Submitted by all pedagogues immediately following *each* absence, along with necessary documentation (e.g., medical notes).
    • OP 201 form: Submitted for days of non-attendance, such as death in family, jury duty, graduation, and professional conferences.
  • Extended Leaves (over 10 days): Generally requires using the Self-Service Online Leave Application System (SOLAS) for non-supervisory pedagogical staff, UFT Paraprofessionals, and DC37 School Personnel.

Chapter 3: Student Discipline & Enrollment

Key Policies and Procedures

  • Removal of Substantially Disruptive Students: Policy for teacher removal of disruptive students (Level 3 infractions). Includes procedures for the Student Removal Form, parental notification, and providing an educational plan.
  • Occurrence Reporting Procedures: Guidelines for reporting student-related occurrences, including emergencies (call 911), incidents involving weapons, drugs, illness, or injury, and procedures when police/EMS are called.

Chapter 4: Student Trip Information

Field Trip Procedures

  • All school-sanctioned trips must be **educational** and relevant to the curriculum; recreational trips are **not** approved.
  • Trip Approval: Requires Borough Principal approval in advance. Teachers must complete a **Trip Plan form** for each trip.

Required Forms (Chapter 4)

  • Parent Notification/Consent Form - Day Trip
  • Trip Plan

Chapter 5: OMNY Card Information

OMNY Card Distribution and Accountability Guidelines

  • Procedures are in place for assigning OMNY cards to students in ATS.
  • An **Invalidation Form** must be completed for students who have been absent 10 consecutive days, discharged, or have lost/damaged their cards.
